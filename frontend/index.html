Why Early Detection Saves Sight

The Global Challenge

Over 415 million people live with diabetes—1 in 3 will develop diabetic retinopathy (DR). In rural and low-resource regions, access to ophthalmologists is limited, leading to delayed diagnosis until irreversible vision loss occurs.

Our AI Solution

RetinaGuard uses a fine-tuned EfficientNet CNN trained on 80,000+ fundus images from EyePACS and APTOS datasets. It classifies DR severity (None, Mild, Moderate, Severe, Proliferative) with >92% sensitivity and specificity.

Privacy & Ethics

All processing occurs in real time. Images are never stored, logged, or transmitted beyond your local inference server. Built for telemedicine, community screening, and point-of-care use.

Technical Details

AI Model Architecture

Built on EfficientNet-B4 backbone, fine-tuned with transfer learning from ImageNet. Features attention mechanisms and multi-scale feature fusion for precise lesion detection. Model size: 75MB, optimized for edge deployment.

Datasets & Training

Trained on 80,000+ annotated fundus images from EyePACS (35,000), APTOS (3,662), and RFMiD (1,920). Includes diverse ethnicities and imaging conditions. Data augmentation: rotations, flips, brightness adjustments.

Performance Metrics

Achieves 94.2% accuracy, 92.8% sensitivity, 95.1% specificity on validation set. AUC-ROC: 0.967. Tested on external datasets with consistent performance across demographics.

Impact & Use Cases

Enables mass screening in underserved areas, reduces ophthalmologist workload by 60%. Used in telemedicine programs, mobile clinics, and primary care settings. Supports early intervention, potentially saving millions from vision loss.
